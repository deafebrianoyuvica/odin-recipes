Indonesian's Satay

Description

Indonesian satay, or "sate," is a beloved dish consisting of skewered and grilled meat served with a flavorful peanut sauce. This iconic street food is a staple of Indonesian cuisine, showcasing the country's rich culinary heritage. The meat, often chicken, beef, or lamb, is marinated in a blend of spices and coconut milk, giving it a tender texture and aromatic flavor. Satay is typically served with a side of rice cakes (ketupat), cucumber slices, and a drizzle of sweet soy sauce. The combination of smoky, savory, and nutty flavors makes Indonesian satay a favorite dish for gatherings, celebrations, or casual meals. Whether enjoyed at a bustling street vendor or prepared at home, satay captures the essence of Indonesia's vibrant food culture.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken, beef, or lamb, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 10-12 bamboo skewers, soaked in water
  • 3 tablespoons coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Peanut sauce for serving
  • Cucumber slices and rice cakes (ketupat) for garnish

      • Steps

      1. In a bowl, combine coconut milk, soy sauce, sweet soy sauce, minced garlic, ground coriander, turmeric powder, ground cumin, salt, and pepper to create the marinade.
      2. Add the meat to the marinade, ensuring it is well coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 1 hour or overnight in the refrigerator.
      3. Thread the marinated meat onto the soaked bamboo skewers.
      4. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium heat.
      5. Grill the skewers, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and slightly charred, about 10-15 minutes.
      6. Serve the satay hot with peanut sauce, cucumber slices, and rice cakes (ketupat) on the side.
